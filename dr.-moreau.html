My self-summary

 

I'm originally from London but I decided to leave that life behind to pursue my passions and dreams. I bought a private island in the pacific and here I am now. Like any sane man, I am religious. I have constantly sought my Maker's laws all my life and I am looking for a girl that will help me to find those laws. I love animals. I don't think that I could live without them! I like animals so much that I almost consider them to be human.

 

What I'm looking for

 

Ideally I would like a girl who is willing to do more than just chase butterflies. I want someone who help me achieve my dreams. Liking animals is a must!

 

What I'm doing with my life

 

As I stated previously I left my life in London behind to pursue my passions and dreams. I am currently on the verge of making breakthroughs in science that the world has never dared to dream of. I was the first man in the world to approach this question armed with antiseptic surgery, and a scientific knowledge of the laws of growth. Critics will say that I am wrong, but I continue on heeding only the question I am pursuing.

How H.G. Wells portrays Dr. Moreau:

 

  • Dr. Moreau is typically considered the villain of the story. He was driven out of London by the scientific community to a deserted island. On this island, he subjects animals to horrific torture without anesthetic and plays the role of God to the beast folk through brainwashing them and using the threat of torture against them.

 

    • “He who breaks the Law, goes back to the house of pain!” –Dr. Moreau (page 70)

 

    • “Still alive, but so cut and mutilated as I pray I may never see living flesh again.” –Predick (about Dr. Moreau’s Puma experiment) (Page 52)

 

My claim:

 

  • Wells never gives readers a chance to feel empathy for Dr. Moreau. He is always portrayed in a bad light, and even his death happens off screen in order to make sure that no pity is felt for him.
