My self-summary

 

I'm originally from London and I just recently ended up on this island after being shipwrecked on the journey of "The Lady Vain". I got my education in biology, specializing in the study of butterflies. I would describe myself as a very honest person that is always trying to do the right thing. I find a lot of satisfaction in helping other people.

 

What I'm looking for

 

 I guess I'm just looking for that special someone that will add something meaningful in my life. I don't know exactly what to look for, but I have a feeling that I will know it when I see it.

 

What I'm doing with my life

 

Back in London I always had plenty of things to do, but these days I am just waiting for a ship to come around to take me home. I like relaxing on the beach, going on long walks, spending time with my new friends that I have made here.

How H.G. Wells Portrays Edward Prendick:

 

  • Edward Prendick is the voice of the story and so the perspective that we are shown by H.G. Wells is, more or less, the same perspective that Prendick has of himself. However, H.G. Wells gives many small clues throughout the book about faults and flaws in Prendick that would be more prevalent if the book were from someone else’s perspective. For example, his black and white view of the world often causes him to jump to irrational conclusions. He is unwilling to trust people, doesn’t think through his decisions, and blames others.

 

    • “What am I doing? —I am going to drown myself.” –Prendick (Page 48)

 

    • “These sickening scoundrels had merely intended to keep me back to fool me with their display of confidence, and presently to fall upon me with a fate more horrible than death.” –Prendick (Page 37)

 

    • “I raised this nailed stick of mine and cut at his face” –Prendick  (Page 37)

 

My claim:

 

  • If the book were told from a different perspective, readers would still think of Edward Prendick as a good man, but more of his faults would be exposed.
