About

 

 

  • Definitions of Humanity

    A famous proverb says “Truth is in the eye of the beholder”. Evidence of this can be seen in “The Island of Dr. Moreau." The novel follows the adventures of a group of people on Dr. Moreau's island where he is conducting ethically questionable experiments on animals. The book is told from the perspective of Edward Prendick, a stranded newcomer to the island. Through his perspective we observe several different characters that come from various backgrounds that all have differing opinions on what is acceptable and what makes someone human. Each of the characters feels passionate about their own views and they sincerely believe that what they are doing is right. These differing definitions on what is right and wrong are a key theme to the novel and they are completely dependent on personal backgrounds and points of view.

     

     

  • Bias of Empathy

    In addition to the characters being biased based on individual background, the empathy that the reader experiences towards each of these characters is manipulated by the perspective taken by H.G. Wells; for example: Dr. Moreau is seen as the villain of the novel because the reader experiences everything from Prendick's view point. I believe that bias in empathy would be remediated if the perspective was changed.

     

  • My Project

    For my project, I wanted to explore how perspective influences the empathy that is felt towards each of the characters. I decided that the most effective way to do this was by making an Online dating website where 5 characters had a profile. The characters that I chose to analyze were Dr. Moreau, Montgomery, Edward Prendick, the ship captain, and the leopard man.

     

    An online dating profile gives a unique, easy to control perspective because everyone is trying to put their best foot forward. On the profile tab of the website you will find profiles for each of the 5 characters listed above. As you read through each of the profiles, try your best to keep an open mind in order to see the character from a different light.

     

    As I was creating the profiles for this project, I was amazed to see how my personal biases affected what I was trying to write. I was influenced by the perspective taken by the author and even when I was trying to be unbiased, I still favored certain characters. That was strong evidence to me of the powerful subconscious effect that what we read has on us.
